## 📋 Executive Summary

A venture-backed startup with a promising product struggled to build a repeatable sales motion. With no defined sales process, inconsistent lead follow-up, and a fragmented CRM, the organization was burning cash without predictable revenue.

By building a structured Go-to-Market (GTM) strategy, implementing a lightweight CRM, and training a small sales team on a repeatable framework, the startup achieved significant revenue growth and built a multi-million dollar pipeline within 18 months.

> **Why this matters to a business:** Startups fail not because of product-market fit — they fail because of sales execution. This case study demonstrates how structured sales operations scale early-stage ventures.

---

## 🎯 The Challenge

- **No defined sales process:** Every rep followed their own approach; results were inconsistent
- **Fragmented lead management:** Leads scattered across spreadsheets, emails, and memory
- **Long sales cycles:** Average 90+ days to close, leading to cash flow pressure
- **No forecasting capability:** Leadership had no visibility into revenue predictability

**The Stakes:** The startup had 18 months of runway. Without a repeatable sales motion, survival was at risk.

---

## 🛠️ My Approach: The Startup Sales Engine

### Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1-4)
1. Audited existing sales activities and identified 5 distinct stages in the customer journey
2. Implemented Salesforce as the single source of truth
3. Created a lead scoring model based on engagement and intent signals

### Phase 2: Process Build (Weeks 5-12)
1. Designed a 5-step sales framework: Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Negotiation → Close
2. Built sales playbooks for each stage (scripts, objection handling, collateral)
3. Established weekly pipeline review cadence with leadership

### Phase 3: Scale & Optimize (Weeks 13-24)
1. Hired and trained 2 additional sales reps using the new framework
2. Implemented sales forecasting model with improved accuracy
3. Created customer success handoff protocol to improve retention

## 💡 Key Learnings

1. **Structure Before Scale:** You cannot scale chaos. Building the right foundation first was the smartest investment.

2. **CRM is Not Optional:** Spreadsheets break at scale. Salesforce gave us the visibility needed to make decisions.

3. **Training is an Investment, Not a Cost:** Reps who followed the playbook consistently outperformed those who didn't.

4. **Forecasting is a Discipline, Not a Guess:** The weekly pipeline review became the heartbeat of the sales organization.
